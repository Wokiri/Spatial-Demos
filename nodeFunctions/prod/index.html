In that endeavor, I must say they did relatively well: in my boyhood, I watched wrestling in a colored TV, or played snake xenxia, or played super mario at home. These things brought a deep sense of satisfaction in a young boy's life.

I then enjoyed the unique possibilities that technology offered. I was moved by these innovations, I admired them and the whole phylosophy behind their creation-- to make life a little easier and sweeter, reasonated largely with my idea of 'good life.' I wanted good life. I desperately craved for more beyond the little that would be traced within our humble abode.

And so it was that my love for technology was cultured.

Today the story remains unchanged. It interests me that mankind at a time that constitutes the present finds his life revolving around some (if not all) aspects of this great technological dynamic. The various forms in which technology has revolutionized within just over two decades is a many sided reality that speaks of nothing but exponential growth and developement of useful applications.

Advancement of the web infrastructure for instance has brought significant alterations to the way the world is run. The internet has becomes man's central medium of communication. It is his conduit to social relations, his means of study, research and education, a successful tool of trade, a potential facilitator of entertainment, just to mention but a few.
